Volcano National Park

Hawaiʻi Volcanoes National Park encompasses two active volcanoes: Kīlauea, one of the world's most active volcanoes, and Mauna Loa, the world's most massive shield volcano. The park provides dramatic volcanic landscapes, glimpses of rare flora and fauna, and a view into the traditional Hawaiian culture connected to these landscapes.

Waipi'o Valley

In the beginning there was Waipiʻo Valley, a place of such sheer beauty that it could only have been conceived by the most benevolent and creative of all the gods of nature. Located north of the town of Honokaʻa along the Hamakua Coast, the valley is the largest and southernmost of the seven valleys on the windward side of the Kohala Mountains.

Star Gaze at Mauna Kea

At 14,000 ft., Mauna Kea is home to some of the best observatories in the world for a reason. Plan a trip up for sunset and be sure to pack plenty of warm clothes! Maunakea, a sacred volcano in Hawaiian culture and arguably the premier place on Earth to view stars. The entire Milky Way and Venus, Saturn, and Jupiter strung out across the sky like pearls can be observed there.
