Cuisine

Poke

Poke is a simple dish made of chopped seafood, generally tuna, marinated in soy sauce and sesame oil, and mixed with onion. But, you’ll find many variations of this when you visit poke shops and grocery stores around Hawaii.

Loco Moco

Loco moco is a dish featured in contemporary Hawaiian cuisine. There are many variations, but the traditional loco moco consists of white rice, topped with a hamburger, a fried egg, and brown gravy.

Pineapple Fried Rice

This Hawaiian pineapple fried rice makes the perfect summer time meal when pineapples are in season! It is full of juicy pineapple chunks, colorful vegetables, meat and cooked rice. Serve it up in hollowed out pineapple bowls for a festive presentation!

Malasadas

Malasadas, as they are known in Hawaii, are a yeast-leavened doughnut enriched with eggs, butter, and sometimes evaporated or fresh milk. After frying, they are rolled in sugar. On the island they can be found with all manner of filling including plain custard and coconut-flavored haupia pudding.

Shave Ice

Hawaiian shave ice is as refreshing as a dip in the Pacific Ocean on a balmy afternoon. In paradise, it’s as prevalent as a good wave and as eye-catching as a coastal sunset. Shave ice absorbs the flavor more evenly thanks to its plethora of super-tiny ice bits. The consistency is fine and pillowy, like freshly fallen snow.

Mai Tai

A Mai Tai is the drink that usually comes to mind when the words “Hawaiian cocktail” are mentioned. Its distinctive multi-colored look comes from using white rum as the base and adding a float of dark rum on top. Orange curaçao and orgeat syrup are also key ingredients, along with fresh juices.
